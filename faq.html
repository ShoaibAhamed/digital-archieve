Frequently Asked Questions(FAQ)

What is Wiley Digital Archives?

Wiley Digital Archives is a program of databases consisting of newly-digitized primary sources, hosted on an intuitive, purpose-built platform that is specifically designed to meet archive research needs. Wiley Digital Archives is digitizing unique and rare primary sources into a discoverable online format, allowing researchers from around the world to access source materials from learned socieites and other kinds of archival repositories. The platform enables access, and tools which engender a deeper context, making it possible for researchers and students to gain a closer understanding, or even reinterpretation, of previously published—and unpublished--- research conclusions.


Why is Wiley digitizing historical archives?

Wiley is giving users an exclusive pathway into extensive and distinct research cultivated by some of the world’s most prominent societies. Additionally, the digitization process will help preserve valuable research for generations to come, while easing the “wear and tear” on the original analog content. This will enable researchers around the world to innovate within their respective fields.


With whom does Wiley partner?

Currently, Wiley Digital Archives has partnerships with five prestigious academic societies:

1. New York Academy of Sciences (NYAS). NYAS has been driving innovative solutions to society’s challenges by advancing scientific research, education, and policy for 200 years. This unique collection spans from ~1801 to ~2013, and is drawn from the NYAS’ holdings in New York City.

2. New York Botanical Garden (NYBG). Two key collection in the NYAS archive are drawn from the holding of the NYBG—the papers of Nathaniel Lord Britton and the Lewis David von Schweinitz papers. Both collections relate to the study of flora and cover the US as well as Puerto Rico and the Virgin Islands.

3. Royal Anthropological Institute of Great Britain and Ireland (RAI). The RAI is committed to promoting a public understanding of anthropology and the contribution of anthropology to public affairs. This collection spans dates from1763-2016, with some material from post-1967 material held back under privacy rules.

4. The Royal College of Physicians (RCP). The RCP is a professional membership body for physicians, with over 33,000 members and fellows around the globe working in hospitals and communities across 30 medical specialties. The RCP collection contains a vast range of original sources that have shaped over six centuries of medical progress, from ~1300- ~1980.

5. (IN PROGRESS) Royal Geographical Society with the Institute of British Geographers (RGS-IBG). Wiley and RGS-IBG are working together with academic advisors, fellows, members, archivists and librarians to identify and organize content for digitization. Several million images will be conserved and scanned, including manuscripts, maps, illustrations, monographs, field notes, meeting minutes, administrative records, proceedings, correspondence, ephemera and other archival content from among the extensive holdings at the RGS-IBG library and archives. The expected chronology will run from ~1700 to ~2000.

How do the society partners benefit from this arrangement? Does the original content return to the society?

Our partners get a free copy of the new digital resource and receive their items returned in stable condition: repaired, if need, be, and housed in new archival-quality storage (boxes, folders) where needed. We also deliver back the images, text and any derived, or enanced metadata. We never restrict access to the analog content, while providing preservation copies of the newly-digitized content back to each partner society.


What is the process behind making these documents available?

First, we partner with well-known societies that have valuable physical archives that are digitally untapped. We send a team of trained conservators, catalogers, and scanning experts to take inventory. These professionals are equipped with state-of-the-art tools to repair, restore, and preserve all documents. After that, we digitize everything for the modern researcher. This includes making documents searchable and browsable, along with tools for visualization, mapping, and textual analysis.

What makes Wiley Digital Archives unique?

The Wiley Digital Archives platform has been specifically designed to meet modern archival research needs. It is a customized platform that has been purposely built for digital archive research, with content that be seamlessly integrated into courseware, syllabi, and learning management systems. The Wiley Digital Archives platform enables users to search, view, translate, download, and share content. Handwritten materials include keyed-in information and metadata from document headers, such as names, places, and dates, to make the manuscript content more discoverable. Printed documents are full-text searchable and can be downloaded as text or images, and the text can be translated.

Tabular data is keyed in and fielded, so items, such as charts, graphs, and datasets, are exportable into a fielded CSV document with one click. This includes handwritten data captured in fieldnotes and elsewhere. Search results are returned visually, rather than simply as a static list, and historical maps are geo-tagged so that a user can locate a document in both place and time. This allows the archive to be searched or accessed by visual browse and refined by metadata fields. In addition, the search engine performs full Boolean search functionality and fuzzy logic searching. You can print and download individual documents, as well as share them via email or social media. When an item has been shared, the recipient will be able to view that document inside the platform without the need for authentication.


What types of administrative and integration features are available?

Wiley Digital Archives uses the MARC standard for cataloging records, preventing duplication of work and allowing libraries to better share predictable and reliable bibliographic resources, including KBART files. MARC is the industry standard for recordkeeping that helps foster the communication of information. We are also COUNTER-compliant, meaning we produce and exchange consistent, credible usage statistics. This allows libraries to compare usage data received from different vendors and publishers. Libraries use the information to better understand and demonstrate the value of the electronic resources they are providing access to. Additionally, Wiley Digital Archives delivers metadata to major search and discovery services, such as Summon® , Primo® and EBSCO Discovery ServiceTM, to promote the helpful exchange of information.

This is done upon completion of the digitization of every full collection. The text (OCR, metadata) and images behind the Wiley Digital Archives program are available to any institution with access to the parent resources, free of charge, for textual analysis and data-mining outside of the resource. Wiley will also deposit a copy of each archive with an accredited third-party repository to guarantee preservation and access. Lastly, administrators can manage their access rights directly through the platform. Administrators can also brand the resource with their own insitution’s logo, and create a link back to general library page (or any institutional page they wish).


How do researchers, educators, and students gain access to this newly-digitized content?

Once an institution has purchased a collection within Wiley Digital Archives, users will gain access to the fully-integrated platform through their library authentication system. If multiple collections are purchased, users can perform cross-collection searches.


How do I purchase Wiley Digital Archives?

Wiley Digital Archives is a one-time purchase per collection. The pricing differs for each archive, based on the value of the content, uniqueness, appeal, size of collection, and other factors, such as FTE. Once purchased, your institution will have continuous access for an unlimited number of users. There are no ongoing fees once the collection is purchased. Wiley gladly offers librarian and researcher training.
