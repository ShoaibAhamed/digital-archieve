Visualization, Exploration and Analysis

The Wiley Digital Archives platform was purpose-built with archival research needs in mind. Navigating through centuries’ worth of content can seem like a daunting task, but Wiley Digital Archives enables researchers to engage with this content in meaningful and efficient ways, with as little as a search term or through complex advanced searches and algorithmic queries. 

Beyond simply searching and browsing content, the analysis, visualization, and exploration tools built inside the platform will provide new ways for researchers to engage with archival content, and will surface content which further contextualizes the direct results of search term queries and place subjects, events, and people into chronologies and geographies. Wiley Digital Archives seeks to offer content to researchers and students in ways the tie directly to research workflows and educational outcomes.

Publication Brower Analysis Hub Explorer tool

The Place of Publication Browser calls attention to where content was published, when, by whom, and on which subjects. Users can to see who was talking about what, where, when, and in which publication mediums, by selecting an area on the map or by executing a search. This assists users in tracing the evolution of ideas over time and geographies, and adds a visual element to a simple “place of publication” search, adding geographical and historical perspective to the content

The Analysis Hub offers statistical analysis tools for Term Frequency andTerm Popularity, which enable users to place their search terms in various contexts over time, and while Term Groups identifies relationships between search terms and the most meaningful subjects, people, locations and events related to those search terms through textual analyses.

The Explorer Tools offer a more visual engagement with the rich collections of photos and maps in the archives. Browse all of the photographs in an archive or collection in a gallery display using the Photos tool, or narrow the images down via a simple search term, or through the advanced search fields and delimiters. The Maps tool also enables users to view maps over time and locations simply by selecting an area of the world. Once a selection is made, all of the maps which correspond to that location (through geocoordinate tagging) will appear in the viewing pane, and users can then click into any map for a more detailed view.

The team at Wiley Digital Archives continues to iterate on our analysis and visualization tools, so look for further pathways and features coming to the platform which seek to uncover connections between archival documents and add deeper meaning and context to the growing primary sources in the Wiley Digital Archives program. .
