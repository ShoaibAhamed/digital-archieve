The Wiley Digital Archives platform is designed to provide online access to primary resources materials to support researchers and students across the globe, and this includes lowering the barriers for access and understanding through the availability of a translatable interface, and through the provision of translation tools for the printed primary source content contained the Archives.

There are two support tools in place to accommodate a range of native-language speakers. The first tool is the translatable platform interface, which allows the user to choose a preferred language from thirty-five options. This tool allows the headers, descriptive text, support information, and platform navigation tools to be translated to the language of your choice, to assist in efficient maneuvering through the materials within the platform.


Learn More

The Wiley Digital Archives platform is currently available in the following languages:

Afrikaans, Arabic, Bahasa (Indonesian), Bahasa (Malaysian), Bengali (Bangladesh and India), Chinese (Traditional), Chinese (Simplified), Croatian, Czech, Danish, Dutch, English, Finnish, French, German, Greek, Hindi, Hungarian, Italian, Japanese, Korean, Polish, Portuguese, Romanian, Russian, Slovakian, Slovenian, Spanish, Swedish, Tagalog, Tamil, Thai, Turkish, Vietnamese, and Welsh.

Training for librarians, faculty, researchers, and students is free, and is gladly offered from Wiley.

The second support tool allows for printed materials to be translated from their native language to a preferred language via Google Translate. Google Translate is a Google-based service which translates words, phrases, and web pages between English and over 100 other languages. Google Translate has been embedded within all aspects of the Wiley Digital Archives platform and can directly provide translations to printed primary resources materials found online, as well as via downloads of entire texts in preferred languages. These translations widen the scope of help available to the user, as the global team can review documents in their native language.
