Needs a headline

Wiley understands the undeniable value that primary source materials have in today’s research and are deeply invested in the preservation of these coveted pieces of research. These one-of-a-kind materials have the capacity to change the way in which we view the world, how we publish, or even reinterpret previously published research.

With our deep appreciation for society-based research and our long-rooted history in publishing academic content, we feel a distinct privilege to play a part in the digital preservation of historic, academic content. By partnering with highly regarded societies from around the world, we can preserve the past, and make this valuable content available and accessible for years to come.

End to End Assistance:

Wiley provides end-to-end assistance and personnel to inventory, identify, catalog, assess, conserve, track, and otherwise prepare a physical collection for digitization and dissemination.

First, our team of trained conservators, catalogers, and scanning experts take inventory to review the documents and determine if each piece of content is able to be scanned and digitized. If they are not in suitable condition, these professionals are equipped with state-of-the-art tools to repair, restore, and preserve all types of documents. A careful restoration process is integral to enable the digital preservation of each piece of content. This includes repairing damaged items and stabilizing the content to safeguard against the wear of time. Project managers and scan operators working on this project have all had extensive training in handling fragile material and rare content. This is an exciting discovery process, and provides Wiley to play a part in preserving the past.

Through this process we not only mitigate the risk associated with physical examination, but we may also improve the condition of the original sources. Archival items are repaired, if need be, and are housed in new, archival-quality storage (boxes, sleeves, folders) where appropriate. These storage materials are provided to the society, courtesy of Wiley. Other conservation activities included: surface cleaning (including mold cleaning), tear repair and fragment re-attachment (if there is text on the fragment), spine consolidation, bolt-slitting, flattening and sleeving fragile items in Mylar (instead of repairing, where appropriate).

Next, cutting-edge technology is used to scan high-res images and perform Optical Character Recognition (OCR), and the header information from handwritten text is keyed-in by hand using a double-key process. A subsequent quality assurance process ensures each piece of content is tagged with the exact folder, box, and collection it came from, preserving the original hierarchy and structure of the physical archive.Once the content is digitized, descriptive metadata, including headers, names, places, and dates, is manually keyed-in and interpolated with existing catalogs or finding aids, to optimize search and discovery. .
