Wiley Digital Archives offers digital access to unique and rare primary source material through collections provided by learned societies and institutes around the world!

The scholarly record is as much defined by the information we have access to as the information we donâ€™t. A journal article from a prestigious society can tell us the scientific methodology, results and conclusions of a significant research study, but what of the story behind it? What historical context shaped the study to begin with? What new perspectives should be considered and how does that effect our interpretation of the literature?

Distinct and deliberate, society archives reflect a comprehensive institutional perspective that illuminate their values, ideas, disagreements, breakthroughs, aspirations and personal member interactions that shaped the pursuit of their scientific goals over time. Professional societies have a long and rich history of conducting research and publishing academic content, which at times, have been the first to explore original ideas and led to landmark discoveries and breakthroughs. Society archives are deep treasure troves of unique and very rare primary source materials, that typically, are only accessible in situ.

Geotagged Historical Maps Unlimited User Access

All the collections within the Wiley Digital Archives platform contain historical maps, a valuable and desirable primary source content type. Every single map included inside each collection has been geo-tagged. This enables researchers to browse through the maps using a visual search tool. Once a researcher has found the map to use, there is the ability to tie each map to its location on the globe.

The Maps tool enables users to view maps over time and locations, with one click. After selecting an area of the world, the maps which correspond to that location (through geocoordinate tagging) will appear in the viewing pane. Users can click into any map for a more detailed view and share with their colleagues via email

Researchers, students and faculty members alike can have an easy, seamless experience while using the Wiley Digital Archives platform. An institutional purchase allows for full-access to an unlimited number of users within the provided IP range. This type of access allows anyone within that range, whether they are on campus, or in their own homes, the ability to connect with this valuable primary source database, no matter their location.

Unique primary source materials that can be found within the collections include personal correspondence, field notes, manuscript maps, meeting minutes, ephemera, grey literature, drawings, photographs and so much more.Digitization of these primary source collections into a discoverable online format enables researchers from around the world to easily access critical source materials that offer a closer understanding and more nuanced interpretation of published works, while encouraging new questions and research pathways. Wiley Digital Archives provide access to the unique and rare primary sources that tell the story behind the contemporary research content we publish today, while unlocking data and content which feed into entirely new areas of research and education.
