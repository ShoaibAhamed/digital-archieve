The MARC data elements make up the foundation of most library online public access catalogs used today!

MARC is the acronym for MAchine-Readable Cataloging. It defines a data format by which computers exchange, use, and interpret bibliographic information. The MARC data elements make up the foundation of most library online public access catalogs used today. These can simply be defined as electronic versions of "cards" in library card catalogs. They are a focal point of monograph discovery and are critical for libraries, who want to help multiple entry points to online book content.

Institutions that have access to Wiley Digital Archives collections are entitled to receive free MARC records. These full-level bibliographic records include key metadata elements that drive discovery, such as: table of contents, summaries, Library of Congress subject headings, call numbers, persistent links and more. Records may be downloaded through the administrator dashboard on the platform.

Discovery Services Webscale discovery services (or discovery layers) are tools that search across institutional collections and provide relevance-ranked results. They are used in libraries around the world to help augment licensed content discovery and provide multiple entrance points to users. The Wiley Digital Archives portal enables COUNTER- and SUSHI-compliant usage statistics, local branding for your library, and insights into how these collections are being accessed and used.Usage statistics and reports are available for download exclusively on the Wiley Digital Archives Administrative Portal. Once logged in, administrators can choose which type of usage report to access (admin.wileydigitalarchives.com), and can customize reports by date range.

Custom Usage Reports straight from the platform Wiley provides detailed usage statistics on both trial and purchased collections, measuring the number of sessions, searches, downloads, and translations across each collection and at the platform level (cross-search).

COUNTER reportsWiley Digital Archives COUNTER 5-compliant and all customers are entitled to access their own COUNTER 5-formatted usage reports via our Admin Portal.

SUSHI Wiley Digital Archive collections are compliant with the SUSHI (Standardized Usage Harvesting Initiative) protocol for automated harvesting and consolidation of usage from different vendors. Go to Admin Portal

KBART is an acronym for KnowledgeBase And Related Tools. It is a NISO ((National Information Standards Organization) recommended practice that allows for a standardized method of displaying title lists. This was originally developed for link resolver vendors. The uses for KBART span widely into electronic resource management. Content integration with vendor knowledgebases is critical. To make workflows easier for customers, Wiley has partnered with library solutions vendors in sharing title lists for its content. While most libraries prefer working in the vendor knowledgebases and aligning their content, we'd also like to provide you with the files themselves for institutional use. You can access title lists for the Wiley Digital Archives collections KBART files.
