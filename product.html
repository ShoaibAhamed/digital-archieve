Digital archives collections in one platform!

Wiley Digital Archives (WDA) is a program of digitized primary sources, including: maps, original manuscripts, periodicals, administrative papers, historical books, photographs, ephemera etc… from the world’s leading societies.

Wiley Digital Archives are offered on an intuitive, purpose-built platform, specifically designed to meet archival research needs while connecting the content to educational outcomes. These collections of digitized, unique and rare primary sources have been compiled into a discoverable online format, allowing researchers from around the world to access source materials. This deeper context enables researchers and students to gain a closer understanding, or even reinterpretation, of previously published research conclusions, and to access unpublished data and research in their raw formats.


Resources include :

Searched for an easy access to specific sources

Browsed to find resources about subjects without knowing the name

Downloaded to use the source in a research

Translated to give the opportunity to anyone to translate on his own language for a better understanding

and Shared to enable historical documents to be used in today’s research

How that helps?

Wiley Digital Archives is a customized platform with various features that will make your research enjoyable. Manuscript content is discoverable by indexing information from document headers, such as names, places, and dates on handwritten materials. Other resources include blueprints, correspondence, meetings, personal papers, photographs and more. With our different features, our platform allows printed documents are full-text searchable and can be downloaded, viewed as text, or translated into multiple language.

Charts, graphs, and datasets are exportable into a fielded CSV document with one click. This includes handwritten data captured in fieldnotes and elsewhere. Search results are returned with visual cues, rather than simply as a static list, and historical maps are geo-tagged enabling researchers and students to browse maps using a visual search tool, tying each map to its location on the globe. You can print and download individual documents, and documents can also be shared via email or social media. Shared items can then be viewed by the recipient without authentication into a library system.

Our Partners:



Wiley Digital Archives is an on-going initiative that will amass primary source content into digitized archival collections, publishing several archives per year onto the same platform. There are currently four archives in the collection:

The New York Academy of Science (NYAS) has been driving innovative solutions to society’s challenges by advancing scientific research, education, and policy for 200 years. The NYAS collection spans from 1801 to 2013.

Royal Anthropological Institute of Great Britain and Ireland (RAI) is committed to promoting a public understanding of anthropology and the contribution of anthropology to public affairs. The RAI collection runs from about 1763 through the 2016.

The Royal College of Physicians (RCP) is a professional membership body for physicians, with over 33,000 members and fellows around the globe working in hospitals and communities across 30 medical specialties. The RCP collection contains a vast range of original sources that have shaped over five centuries of medical progress and contains both archival and library content, with manuscripts spanning back to 1300..

The Royal Geographical Society with the Institute of British Geographers (RGS-IBG) is currently working with Wiley and academic advisors, fellows, members, archivists and librarians to identify, organize, and conserve/repair content from the RGS collection, and digitization is already underway.

Several million antique books, historical images, maps etc… will be conserved and scanned into the Wiley Digital Archives database. We are continuously working on solidifying other partnerships so that we may make the archives of other leading societies digitally accessible and discoverable
